The 1988 Brazilian Federal Constitution introduced a series of fundamental 
socioeconomic rights and made them subject to judicial enforcement. When the 
Executive and Legislative branches fail to satisfactorily provide these citizen 
prerogatives ÔÇö such as the rights to health, education, or dignified workÔÇöthe Judiciary 
is called upon to correct these state shortcomings. This project aims to investigate which 
socioeconomic factors help explain the annual fluctuations in lawsuits seeking to fulfill 
social rights claims in the state of Rio Grande do Norte. The research question is: How 
does the annual volume of lawsuits filed to secure socioeconomic rights relate to the 
social development indicators of municipalities in Rio Grande do Norte? This study is 
crucial for two reasons: 1) to diagnose the key elements that prompt individuals to seek 
judicial intervention for the fulfillment of basic social provisions; and 2) to expand the 
available knowledge about the judicialization of rights in a state located in a peripheral 
region of Brazil. Our working hypothesis is that there is a significant negative 
correlation between a higher volume of lawsuits seeking socioeconomic claims and 
satisfactory social indicators in the municipalities. In other words, the worse the social 
conditions observed in the indicators, the greater the recourse to the Judiciary, as 
measured by the annual number of lawsuits. The study will focus on the five-year period 
from 2020 to 2024. We will employ a quantitative empirical methodology by 
cross-referencing databases provided by the Judiciary on the judicialization of social 
rights with those from regulatory agencies or civil society concerning municipal social 
indicators.
